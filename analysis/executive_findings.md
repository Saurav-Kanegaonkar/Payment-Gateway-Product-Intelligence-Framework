# Executive Findings

## What Was Analyzed

The artifact analyzes 120 days of synthetic payment gateway operating data across eight partner portfolios, six channels, five payment methods, and four processor routes. It joins transaction KPIs, data quality incidents, reconciliation checks, experiment candidates, and forecast outputs.

## Findings

- The modeled gateway processed $75.1M in payment volume and 1.23M transactions with a weighted authorization rate of 91.8%.
- The highest ranked product opportunity is unattended card acceptance, driven by a 316.9 bps authorization gap and measurable reporting quality risk.
- The top transaction integrity risk is processor latency owned by support operations, with 32 incidents and $9.5K in modeled volume at risk.
- ACH return rate is 1.90%, which makes ACH reliability and settlement confidence a distinct operating review topic rather than a generic payment method metric.
- Smart routing is the highest priority experiment because it has the largest eligible volume, clear authorization-rate measurement, and ready instrumentation.

## Recommendation

Use the workbench as the recurring product analytics artifact for monthly gateway performance review. Product managers should start with the opportunity queue, data and finance partners should inspect the integrity queue, and the roadmap review should use the experiment lab to decide which tests are ready for quarterly OKRs.
